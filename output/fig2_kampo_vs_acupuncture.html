Kampo vs Acupuncture: What Conditions Are Studied?

Butterfly chart comparing disease/symptom coverage across 2,653 articles from two core Japanese traditional medicine journals (1982–2024)

Kampo (漢方) — Journal of Kampo Medicine, n=2,003
Acupuncture (鍼灸) — Both journals combined, n=771
Kampo-dominant conditions (orange): GI diseases, respiratory, metabolic — reflecting that Kampo is prescribed by physicians with access to internal medicine patients.
Acupuncture-dominant conditions (cyan): Sports, back pain, shoulder pain — reflecting the musculoskeletal focus of acupuncture practice.
Shared conditions: Cancer/palliative care, facial palsy, headache, mental health — where both modalities contribute and integration may benefit patients.

Article counts are based on title keyword matching. A single article may appear in multiple categories.
Acupuncture counts include articles from both the Journal of the Japan Society of Acupuncture and Moxibustion (n=650) and acupuncture-related articles in the Journal of Kampo Medicine (n=121).
Only 44 authors published in both journals, highlighting the personnel divide between the two fields.
